Approach

Content is infrastructure. Governance is the architecture that makes it scale.

I came to content strategy through writing — poetry and long-form fiction — which means I've always understood that precision in language isn't cosmetic. Words are the load-bearing walls of any system designed for humans to navigate.

My work has moved from execution toward architecture: how do you build content systems that hold their shape across teams, tools, and AI-augmented workflows? How do you govern voice without calcifying it? How do you design for the model as well as the user?

The transition from senior practitioner to AI content architect isn't a pivot — it's a longer arc becoming visible. I'm interested in owning the content layer as a transformation function: where editorial judgment, systems thinking, and emerging technology meet.

I operate best in the space between the blank page and the production pipeline — where strategic questions still have enough room to matter.

About

I'm a Senior Manager of UX Content Strategy at Charles Schwab, where I lead AI content governance, enterprise writing standards, and talent architecture for a matrixed creative organization.

My work sits at an inflection point: the moment when content stopped being a production output and started being an infrastructure problem. The questions I find most interesting now are about governance at scale — how do you design systems that preserve judgment while enabling velocity?

Before Schwab, I held content leadership roles at Wells Fargo and McKesson. I hold a BA from the University of Dallas and an MA in Professional Writing from Carnegie Mellon, where I focused on the architecture of communication — how structure shapes meaning before a reader ever arrives.

Outside of work, I write poetry and fiction. I think the discipline of writing literary work — the demand for precision, compression, and earned resonance — is deeply related to what makes content systems function at their best.
