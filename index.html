Approach

Good content strategy makes complex systems easier to use and easier to scale.

My work focuses on the content layer of enterprise products: the language, structure, and standards that determine whether information is clear, consistent, and actionable.

I build systems that teams can actually use. That means writing standards with enough specificity to guide real decisions, information architecture that reflects how users think, and governance frameworks that hold up inside real organizational constraints.

I've spent the past decade working at financial services and healthcare companies where content complexity is high and the cost of confusion is measurable. I'm most useful when an organization needs to move from inconsistent, ad hoc content to a system with structure and rules.

I'm currently at Charles Schwab, where I lead UX content strategy and AI content governance for a matrixed product organization.
